Cosmic Fireflies

Scientific Name:

Luminis Noctis

Discovered by / at:

Hyounsuk Ock, New York University Shanghai, 2026

Family:

This creature belongs to a fictional family of night insects that glow softly in dark grass fields. They are shy, sensitive to movement, and often hide close to the ground before rising into the air.

Habitat:

Luminis Noctis lives in quiet meadows under deep blue night skies. It prefers places with tall grass, moving shadows, and a few distant stars.

Appearance:

These fireflies have a soft yellow-green glow. From far away, they look like floating lights, but up close they react to movement and quickly drift away from danger before slowly returning to rest.

Key Characteristics:

  • Glows in the dark.
  • Rests in the grass.
  • Flies away when disturbed.
  • Returns after a short time.

What we know so far about Luminis Noctis? Luminis Noctis is a fictional generative creature inspired by fireflies at night. In this project, the creature begins quietly in the grass and reacts when the viewer approaches. The movement of the grass can disturb the fireflies, causing them to rise, drift, and glow in the air before settling back down. This behavior is meant to create a small interactive ecosystem rather than a single moving object.

The project focuses on atmosphere, repetition, and gentle interaction. The dark gradient sky, stars, and meteor help build a calm nighttime environment, while the fireflies add life and motion. Instead of acting in exactly the same way, each firefly has its own timing and position, which makes the scene feel more alive.

Guidelines for human interaction with this creature Move your mouse near the grass to disturb the environment. Fireflies resting nearby will react, fly away for a while, and then slowly return to their original place. If you move the mouse directly toward them, their glow fades and they try to avoid you.
